Charticulator
The dataset I chose is about school shootings from 2009-2018. I found this data set from a CNN news article about school shootings over 10 years. It has numerous variables that are categorical and numerical. I thought this dataset was interesting, especially because of recent news about school shootings in the past few years. I was interested in learning more about when and where these shootings are prominent. So my central question was: when and where are school shootings happening? The first graph I made using Charticulator. I graphed the states where shootings occurred on the x-axis and the number of victims on the y-axis. This helped me learn which states had more shootings than others. From the graph, we can tell that Florida, Ohio, Illinois, and Texas were among the states with the highest number of shootings. Additionally, since we have been hearing a lot about school shootings in recent years I decided to add an additional factor of year to this plot. The x marks are color coded from light red to dark red. Each year has a color and the colors get darker as you move toward recent years. When looking at the plot, you can tell that there are significantly more dark x’s than light ones. This tells us that there are more shootings in recent years than in the past. We can conclude that shootings are increasing over time.
The second graph I made using Altair. On this graph, I made the y-axis the type of school whether it was elementary, middle, high, etc. Then I made the x-axis the total number of victims for that school type. At first glance, this graph answers our question of where school shootings are most prominent and we can immediately see that most shootings happen in high schools. This is probably because it is easier to gain access to firearms as you grow older. I also wanted to show how many victims there are in each shooting. The bars are shaded light to dark blue based on the number of victims in each shooting, light meaning few and dark meaning a lot. Looking at the graph we can tell that most shootings, regardless of school, only have a few victims. It is interesting to look at the elementary school bar as there are either very few victims or a lot of victims, there isn’t really an apparent in-between.

Google Sheets
The third chart I made using Google Sheets also addresses where school shootings occur the most. This is a pie chart that aims to show the percentages of shootings that happen in different areas. The three areas that were listed in the dataset are urban, suburbtown, and rural. Each piece of the pie is shaded a different color depending on the area and percentages are given to the side. 14% of shootings happen in rural areas, 41% in suburban areas, and 45% in urban areas. We can conclude that more shootings happen in urban areas than in rural areas.
The fourth graph I made using Excel. I addressed the years in the first graph, however, I wanted to see if there was a significant trend over time. To better answer the question of when the shootings occurred, I created a line graph. I graphed the year on the x-axis and the number of victims on the y-axis. I also listed the total number of victims next to each data point to help the reader understand it better. There is a peak in 2012 and 2016. The graph ends in a positive pattern toward the end. This confirms the statement from the first graph that school shootings are increasing over time.
